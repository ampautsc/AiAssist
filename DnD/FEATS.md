# Player's Handbook (2014) — General Feats

*The original 42 feats that launched 5th Edition. Many were revised in the 2024 PHB.*

---

### Actor ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | General |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- Increase your Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20
- You have advantage on Charisma (Deception) and Charisma (Performance) checks when trying to pass yourself off as a different person
- You can mimic the speech of another person or the sounds made by other creatures. You must have heard the person speaking, or heard the creature make the sound, for at least 1 minute

---

### Alert ⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | General |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You gain a +5 bonus to initiative
- You can't be surprised while you are conscious
- Other creatures don't gain advantage on attack rolls against you as a result of being unseen by you

**Notes:** One of the strongest feats in the game. Going first in combat is incredibly powerful. The 2024 version changes this to proficiency-based initiative bonus and the ability to swap initiative with a willing ally.

---

### Athlete ⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | General |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20
- When you are prone, standing up uses only 5 feet of your movement, instead of half your speed
- Climbing doesn't cost you extra movement
- You can make a running long jump or a running high jump after moving only 5 feet on foot, rather than 10 feet

### Charger ⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- When you use your action to Dash, you can use a bonus action to make one melee weapon attack or to shove a creature
- If you move at least 10 feet in a straight line immediately before taking this bonus action, you either gain a +5 bonus to the attack's damage roll (if you chose to make a melee attack and hit) or push the target up to 10 feet away from you (if you chose to shove and you succeed)

### Crossbow Expert ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Ranged) |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You ignore the loading quality of crossbows with which you are proficient
- Being within 5 feet of a hostile creature doesn't impose disadvantage on your ranged attack rolls
- When you use the Attack action and attack with a one-handed weapon, you can use a bonus action to attack with a hand crossbow you are holding

**Notes:** Core feat for ranged builds. Pairs exceptionally well with Sharpshooter. The bonus action attack effectively gives martial characters an extra attack every turn.

---

### Defensive Duelist ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Defense) |
| **Prerequisites** | Dexterity 13 or higher |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- When you are wielding a finesse weapon with which you are proficient and another creature hits you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction to add your proficiency bonus to your AC for that attack, potentially causing the attack to miss you

**Notes:** Scales well with level as proficiency bonus increases. Excellent for Rogues and Dexterity-based fighters.

### Dungeon Delver ⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Exploration |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | Not in PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Intelligence (Investigation) checks made to detect the presence of secret doors
- You have advantage on saving throws made to avoid or resist traps
- You have resistance to damage dealt by traps
- Traveling at a fast pace doesn't impose the normal −5 penalty to your passive Wisdom (Perception) score

**Notes:** Extremely campaign-dependent. In a dungeon-heavy game this is useful; otherwise it's a wasted feat. Removed from the 2024 PHB.

### Elemental Adept ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Spellcasting |
| **Prerequisites** | The ability to cast at least one spell |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- When you cast a spell that deals damage of the chosen type (acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder), you can treat any 1 on a damage die as a 2
- Spells you cast ignore resistance to damage of the chosen type
- You can select this feat multiple times. Each time you do so, you must choose a different damage type

**Notes:** Best for fire damage casters (most spells, most resistance). Ignoring resistance is the main draw.

### Grappler ⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat |
| **Prerequisites** | Strength 13 or higher |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 (significantly improved) |

**Benefits:**
- You have advantage on attack rolls against a creature you are grappling
- You can use your action to try to pin a creature grappled by you. To do so, make another grapple check. If you succeed, you and the creature are both restrained until the grapple ends

**Notes:** The 2014 version is widely considered one of the weakest feats. The 2024 version is substantially better, adding punch-and-grab combos.

### Great Weapon Master ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Melee) |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 (significant changes) |

**Benefits:**
- On your turn, when you score a critical hit with a melee weapon or reduce a creature to 0 hit points with one, you can make one melee weapon attack as a bonus action
- Before you make a melee attack with a heavy weapon that you are proficient with, you can choose to take a -5 penalty to the attack roll. If the attack hits, you add +10 to the attack's damage

**Notes:** The quintessential damage feat for melee characters. The -5/+10 mechanic was removed in the 2024 version, replaced by adding your proficiency bonus to damage once per turn. Core feat for Barbarians, Fighters, and Paladins using two-handed weapons.

### Healer ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Utility |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- When you use a healer's kit to stabilize a dying creature, that creature also regains 1 hit point
- As an action, you can spend one use of a healer's kit to tend to a creature and restore 1d6 + 4 hit points to it, plus additional hit points equal to the creature's maximum number of Hit Dice. The creature can't regain hit points from this feat again until it finishes a short or long rest

**Notes:** Provides non-magical healing that scales decently. Excellent for parties without a dedicated healer.

### Heavy Armor Master ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Defense |
| **Prerequisites** | Proficiency with heavy armor |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- Increase your Strength score by 1, to a maximum of 20

### Inspiring Leader ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Utility/Social |
| **Prerequisites** | Charisma 13 or higher |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You can spend 10 minutes inspiring your companions, shoring up their resolve to fight. When you do so, choose up to six friendly creatures (which can include yourself) within 30 feet of you who can see or hear you and who can understand you. Each creature can gain temporary hit points equal to your level + your Charisma modifier. A creature can't gain temporary hit points from this feat again until it has finished a short or long rest

**Notes:** Excellent for Bards, Paladins, and high-Charisma characters. The temp HP pool is substantial at higher levels.

### Lucky ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | General |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You have 3 luck points. Whenever you make an attack roll, an ability check, or a saving throw, you can spend one luck point to roll an additional d20. You can choose to spend one of your luck points after you roll the die, but before the outcome is determined. You choose which of the d20s is used for the attack roll, ability check, or saving throw
- You can also spend one luck point when an attack roll is made against you. Roll a d20, and then choose whether the attack uses the attacker's roll or yours
- You regain your expended luck points when you finish a long rest

**Notes:** Often considered the single best feat in the game. Works on attacks, saves, AND ability checks. Can effectively negate critical hits against you. The 2024 version uses Heroic Inspiration instead.

### Magic Initiate ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Spellcasting |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- Choose a class: bard, cleric, druid, sorcerer, warlock, or wizard
- You learn two cantrips of your choice from that class's spell list
- In addition, choose one 1st-level spell from that same list. You learn that spell and can cast it at its lowest level. Once you cast it, you must finish a long rest before you can cast it again
- Your spellcasting ability for these spells depends on the class you chose

**Notes:** The 2024 version allows the spell to be cast using existing spell slots and is repeatable (can take it multiple times).

### Martial Adept ⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | Not in PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You learn two maneuvers of your choice from among those available to the Battle Master archetype
- If a maneuver you use requires your target to make a saving throw to resist the maneuver's effects, the saving throw DC equals 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier
- You gain one superiority die, which is a d6. This die is used to fuel your maneuvers. It is expended when you use it, and is regained when you finish a short or long rest

**Notes:** The single d6 superiority die makes this quite weak. Much better for actual Battle Masters who gain extra dice. Removed from 2024 PHB.

### Mounted Combatant ⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You have advantage on melee attack rolls against any unmounted creature that is smaller than your mount
- You can force an attack targeted at your mount to target you instead
- If your mount is subjected to an effect that allows it to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, it instead takes no damage if it succeeds on the saving throw, and only half damage if it fails

**Notes:** Very strong in the specific context of mounted combat, but mounts are fragile and often impractical in dungeons.

### Polearm Master ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Melee) |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- When you take the Attack action and attack with only a glaive, halberd, quarterstaff, or spear, you can use a bonus action to make a melee attack with the opposite end of the weapon. This attack uses the same ability modifier as the primary attack. The weapon's damage die for this attack is a d4, and the attack deals bludgeoning damage
- While you are wielding a glaive, halberd, pike, quarterstaff, or spear, other creatures provoke an opportunity attack from you when they enter your reach

**Notes:** The "enter your reach" opportunity attack is what makes this feat incredible. Combined with Sentinel, enemies literally cannot reach you. One of the best feat combos in the game.

---

### Resilient ⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Defense |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- Increase the chosen ability score by 1, to a maximum of 20
- You gain proficiency in saving throws using the chosen ability

**Notes:** Resilient (Constitution) is the most common choice, especially for spellcasters who need to maintain concentration. Resilient (Wisdom) is also strong for classes that lack Wisdom save proficiency. Scales with proficiency bonus over the campaign.

### Ritual Caster ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Spellcasting |
| **Prerequisites** | Intelligence or Wisdom 13 or higher |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- You have acquired a ritual book holding two 1st-level spells of your choice from a chosen class's spell list. The spells must have the ritual tag
- If you come across a spell in written form (such as a magical spell scroll or a wizard's spellbook), you might be able to add it to your ritual book if the spell is on the spell list for the class you chose, the spell's level is no higher than half your level (rounded up), and if you can spare the time and gold to transcribe the spell
- You can cast ritual spells from the book without having them prepared. The casting time increases by 10 minutes

**Notes:** Gives any character access to powerful utility rituals like Detect Magic, Identify, Find Familiar (Wizard list), Speak with Animals, etc.

| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**

### Sentinel ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Control) |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**
- When you hit a creature with an opportunity attack, the creature's speed becomes 0 for the rest of the turn
- Creatures provoke opportunity attacks from you even if they take the Disengage action before leaving your reach
- When a creature within 5 feet of you makes an attack against a target other than you (and that target doesn't have this feat), you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against the attacking creature

**Notes:** The best "tank" feat. Combined with Polearm Master, you can lock down enemies before they even reach your allies. Essential for battlefield control melee builds.

---

### Sharpshooter ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Ranged) |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 (significant changes) |

**Benefits:**
- Attacking at long range doesn't impose disadvantage on your ranged weapon attack rolls
- Your ranged weapon attacks ignore half cover and three-quarters cover
- Before you make an attack with a ranged weapon that you are proficient with, you can choose to take a -5 penalty to the attack roll. If the attack hits, you add +10 to the attack's damage

**Notes:** The ranged counterpart to Great Weapon Master. The -5/+10 mechanic was removed in the 2024 version. Core feat for any ranged weapon build.

---

### Shield Master ⭐⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Source** | Player's Handbook (2014) |
| **Category** | Combat (Defense) |
| **Prerequisites** | None |
| **Revised In** | PHB 2024 |

**Benefits:**

# Appendix: Edition Comparison — Key Feat Changes (2014 → 2024)

| Feat | 2014 Version | 2024 Version | Impact |
|------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| **Great Weapon Master** | -5/+10 damage option | +proficiency bonus damage (1/turn) | More consistent, lower ceiling |
| **Sharpshooter** | -5/+10 damage option | No damage trade mechanic | Significantly weaker for damage |
| **Lucky** | 3 luck points, reroll d20s | Gain Heroic Inspiration on nat 1 | Completely different mechanic |
| **Alert** | +5 Initiative, can't be surprised | Prof bonus to Initiative, swap Initiative | Changed from flat to scaling |
| **Sentinel** | Speed to 0, ignore Disengage, reaction attack | Speed to 0, reaction "Halt" attack | Slightly simplified |
| **Grappler** | Pin (restrain both), advantage on grappled | Punch and grab combo, Fast Wrestler | Dramatically improved |
| **Tavern Brawler** | +1 ASI, d4 unarmed, grapple on hit | Reroll damage, push, improvised | Became an Origin feat, much improved |
| **Keen Mind** | Know north/time, photographic memory | Lore Knowledge, Quick Study | Now has mechanical benefits |
| **Heavy Armor Master** | -3 nonmagical B/P/S | -Prof bonus all nonmagical B/P/S | Now scales with level |
| **Skulker** | Hide in light obscurement, miss = hidden | Blindsight, fog immunity, sniper | Significantly buffed |
| **Mobile** | +10 speed, disengage on attack | Renamed "Speedy," similar benefits | Name change, minor tweaks |

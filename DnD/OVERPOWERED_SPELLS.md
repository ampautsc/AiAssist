# Overpowered and Controversial D&D 5e Spells (2014 Ruleset)

This document catalogs spells widely considered "overpowered," "broken," or "controversial" within the D&D 5th Edition community and optimization circles. These spells often warp campaign balance, trivialize encounters, or abuse action economy.

## Cantrips

### Guidance (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Constant minimal spam.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** It adds 1d4 to *any* ability check. Because it's a cantrip with no cost other than concentration, it creates a gameplay loop where players shout "I cast Guidance!" before every single check out of combat, effectively giving the party a permanent +2.5 average bonus to everything.

### Eldritch Blast (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** The baseline for all damage.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Not broken on its own, but when combined with the *Agonizing Blast* invocation, it provides the highest consistent resourceless damage in the game (1d10 + CHA mod per beam). It scales with character level, not class level, making it the premier dip for any Charisma caster (Sorlock, Paladin/Warlock).

## 1st Level

### Silvery Barbs (SCoC)
*   **Source:** Strixhaven: A Curriculum of Chaos
*   **The Issue:** Reaction speed capability to force re-rolls.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** This is arguably the most banned spell in 5e. As a *Reaction*, you can force an enemy to reroll a successful attack, check, or save (taking the lower result) AND give advantage to an ally. It effectively serves as a "Legendary Resistance" stripper for players, can negate critical hits, and costs only a 1st-level slot. It fundamentally alters the math of the game too cheaply.

### Shield (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Bounded Accuracy breaker.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** +5 AC as a reaction until the start of your next turn is massive in a system bound by "Bounded Accuracy." A wizard with Mage Armor and Shield can easily have higher AC than a fighter in plate mail. It makes squishy casters tankier than martials for a low resource cost.

### Goodberry (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Trivializes survival.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** One slot specifically solves the entire "survival" pillar of the game (food) and provides incredibly efficient out-of-combat healing (10 HP distributed as needed). Optimized "Life Cleric" builds (with a Druid dip or feat) make each berry heal 4 HP (40 HP total for a 1st level slot), completely breaking early game healing economy.

## 2nd Level

### Pass Without Trace (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Auto-success on stealth.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** +10 to Stealth for the *entire party*. In a d20 system, +10 is mathematically absurd. It turns the heavy-armor Paladin into a ninja. It effectively removes the risk of stealth missions, allowing the party to almost always achieve surprise rounds (which are devastating).

### Web (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** The best crowd control for its level.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Creates difficult terrain and restrains targets on a DEX save. Restrained gives disadvantage on DEX saves and attacks, and gives attackers advantage. It remains relevant all the way to level 20 because it forces actions to escape, wasting enemy turns efficiently.

### Spike Growth (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** The "Cheese Grater."
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Deals 2d4 damage for every 5 feet moved. When combined with forced movement (Grapplers, Repelling Blast, Thorn Whip), it deals astronomical damage without a save.

## 3rd Level

### Conjure Animals (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Action Economy destruction.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Summoning 8 wolves (or velociraptors, cows, etc.) clogs the battlefield, providing 8 huge bags of hit points and 8 attacks (often with Advantage via Pack Tactics). It slows combat to a crawl and mathematically out-damages almost anything else at this level. It forces the DM to deal with 9 entities instead of 1.

### Hypnotic Pattern (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Encounter ender.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** One failed Wisdom save incapacitates enemies for a minute. If you catch half the enemy group, you can defeat the other half and then kill the incapacitated ones one by one. It essentially splits a deadly encounter into two easy ones with a single action.

### Spirit Guardians (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** The Cleric lawnmower.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Lasts 10 minutes, differentiates friend from foe (unlike Fireball), creates difficult terrain, and deals damage every round. It is the gold standard for sustained damage and area control, turning the Cleric into a frontline DPS tank.

### Counterspell (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Stops the game.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** A reaction to simply say "No" to the DM's boss monster casting their big spell. It forces an "arms race" where DMs must give enemies Counterspell, leading to un-fun chains of "I Counterspell your Counterspell."

### Tiny Hut (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Risk-free resting.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** A ritual (no spell slot needed) that creates an impenetrable dome. It trivializes resting in dangerous territory and can be abused in combat setups (popping out to shoot, popping back in) if not strictly adjudicated by the DM.

## 4th Level

### Polymorph (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Massive HP buffer.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Turning a dying ally into a Giant Ape (CR 7) or T-Rex (CR 8) instantly gives them 100+ hit points and huge damage. It is both the best healing spell (by preventing death via temp HP) and a great buff/debuff.

### Conjure Woodland Beings (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Pixie abuse.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** If the DM allows the player to choose the summons (or chooses poorly), summoning 8 Pixies allows them to cast *Polymorph*, *Fly*, or *Confusion* 8 times. 8 T-Rexes appearing from one 4th level slot is the definition of broken.

## 5th Level

### Wall of Force (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** No save, just lose.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** It creates an invisible, indestructible wall. You can trap a boss monster inside (no save allowed) and then ignore them while you kill their minions. Or cook them inside with ongoing damage effects that don't require line of effect (like *Sickening Radiance* - the "Microwave" combo). Most enemies have literally no counterplay to this.

### Animate Objects (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Damage output and physical obstruction.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Animating 10 tiny objects (coins, daggers) gives +8 to hit and deals 1d4+4 damage each. That's 10 attacks per round. The math beats almost every martial character's output and clogs the board with targets.

## 7th Level

### Forcecage (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** *Wall of Force* but better.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Like *Wall of Force*, but lasts 1 hour and doesn't require Concentration (for the cage version). You can trap a boss, leave, take a short rest, and come back. Or just shoot them through the bars while they can't reach you. If the enemy doesn't have teleportation (and passes a CHA save), they automatically lose the fight.

### Simulacrum (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Infinite power glitch.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Creates a copy of yourself with all your spell slots. You instantly double your actions and resources.
*   **The "Chain":** A Wizard casts *Simulacrum*. The Simulacrum (who has slots) casts *Wish* to replicate *Simulacrum* on the Wizard again. This creates an infinite chain of clones (Adventure League bans this specifically). Even without the infinite chain, having two high-level Wizards is unstoppable.

## 9th Level

### Wish (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Literally alters reality.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Aside from the "do anything the DM allows" aspect, its basic function allows you to cast *any* spell of 8th level or lower from *any* class list instantly, without components (ignoring expensive gold costs). This flexibility is unmatched. Casting *Clone* or *Simulacrum* instantly for free is standard operating procedure.

### True Polymorph (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Permanent transformation.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** Turn a rock into a CR 9 monster. Turn yourself into a Dragon permanently. Turn the villain into a chair permanently. It allows players to build armies of high CR monsters given enough downtime.

### Prismatic Wall (PHB)
*   **Source:** Player's Handbook
*   **The Issue:** Invulnerability and damage.
*   **Why it's overpowered:** It's a sphere that blocks everything (spells, attacks, vision). You can stand inside it and be invincible, or shove enemies through it (via *Reverse Gravity* or Telekinesis) to force them to take ~50d6 damage multiple times.

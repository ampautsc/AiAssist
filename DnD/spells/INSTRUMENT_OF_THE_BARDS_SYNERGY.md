# Instrument of the Bards: Spells with Disadvantage

One of the most powerful features of an **Instrument of the Bards** (Uncommon to Legendary versions) is its ability to impose disadvantage on saving throws against **Charm** effects.

> **"You can play the instrument while casting a spell that causes any of its targets to be charmed on a failed save, thereby imposing disadvantage on the save."**

This document lists all spells on the Bard spell list (and key Magical Secrets) that trigger this effect.

---

## The "Golden" Synergies (Top Tier)

These spells are the primary reason to attune to an Instrument. The disadvantage makes them incredibly reliable.

### Hypnotic Pattern (3rd Level)
*   **Effect:** Creatures in a 30ft cube make a Wisdom save or become **Charmed** (incapacitated/speed 0).
*   **Why it's #1:** One save can end an entire encounter. With disadvantage, the probability of failure skyrockets. This is the single best use of the Instrument.

### Suggestion (2nd Level) - *DM Ruling Required*
*   **Effect:** Target makes a Wisdom save or follows a command.
*   **The Trap:** The spell description does **NOT** say the target is "Charmed." It only says they follow the suggestion.
*   **Verdict:** **DOES NOT WORK RAW.** However, many DMs rule that it *should* work because it's a charm-like effect. Ask your DM.

### Dominate Person (5th Level) / Dominate Monster (8th Level)
*   **Effect:** Wisdom save or target is **Charmed**.
*   **Why it's huge:** Total control of an enemy. The initial save is the hardest part; disadvantage makes it much more likely to land.

## Spell List by Level

### Level 1
*   **Animal Friendship:** Target makes Wisdom save or is **Charmed**.
*   **Charm Person:** Target makes Wisdom save or is **Charmed**.

### Level 2
*   **Crown of Madness:** Target makes Wisdom save or is **Charmed**.
*   **Suggestion:** (See above - likely NO).

### Level 3
*   **Fast Friends** (Acq. Inc / Wildemount): Target makes Wisdom save or is **Charmed**.
*   **Hypnotic Pattern:** The MVP.
*   **Nathair's Mischief** (Fizban's): One of the random effects causes the **Charmed** condition.

### Level 4
*   **Charm Monster:** Target makes Wisdom save or is **Charmed**.
*   **Confuson:** NO (does not cause Charmed).

### Level 5
*   **Dominate Person:** Target is **Charmed**.
*   **Geas:** Target is **Charmed**.
*   **Modify Memory:** Target is **Charmed**.

### Level 6
*   **Mass Suggestion:** NO (does not cause Charmed, unlike earlier editions).
*   **Otto's Irresistible Dance:** NO (does not cause Charmed).

### Level 8
*   **Dominate Monster:** Target is **Charmed**.

### Level 9
*   **Psychic Scream:** NO (Stunned, not Charmed).

## Magical Secrets Candidates

If you are a Lore Bard (or wait until Level 10), you can pick up these spells from other lists to use with your Instrument:

1.  **Enthrall (Level 2 - Warlock/Paladin):** Technically Bard too. Causes disadvantage on Perception checks, but not Charmed condition directly in a way that matters for the save itself (it's weird). Skip.
2.  **Wrathful Smite (Level 1 - Paladin):** Frightened, not Charmed.
3.  **Command (Level 1):** No Charmed condition.

---

## Summary for Build Strategy

If you have an Instrument of the Bards, your "Go-To" combat rotation should revolve around **Hypnotic Pattern**. It becomes mathematically one of the strongest crowd control spells in the game.

**Your Spell List Priorities:**
1.  *Hypnotic Pattern* (Must-have)
2.  *Charm Monster* (Non-humanoid removal)
3.  *Dominate Person* (Turning the boss's lieutenant against them)
4.  *Modify Memory* (For narrative/social "wins")

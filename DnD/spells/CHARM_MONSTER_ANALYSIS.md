# Charm Monster (XGtE) - Target Analysis

### The Basics
*   **Target:** "One creature you can see within range."
*   **Range:** 30 feet.
*   **Saving Throw:** Wisdom.
*   **Duration:** Concentration, up to 1 hour.

### "Creature" vs. "Humanoid"
The key distinction is that **Charm Person** targets only creatures with the **Humanoid** type (e.g., humans, elves, dwarves, goblins, orcs). **Charm Monster** targets *any* **Creature**, which includes **Humanoids** and everything else:

*   **Humanoids** (Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Orcs, Goblins) - *Yes, it works on people too!*
*   **Beasts** (Wolves, Bears)
*   **Giants** (Ogres, Trolls)
*   **Monstrosities** (Owlbears, Mimics)
*   **Dragons** (Wyverns, Young Dragons)
*   **Fey** (Hags, Satyrs)
*   **Fiends** (Devils, Demons)
*   **Celestials** (Angels, Pegasi)
*   **Elementals** (Mephits, Gargoyles)
*   **Aberrations** (Mind Flayers, Beholders)
*   **Oozes, Plants, Undead, Constructs** (See immunities below)

### Important Limitations

1.  **Combat:** If you or your companions are fighting the creature, it has **Advantage** on the saving throw.
    *   *Instrument of the Bards Interaction:* If you use an Instrument of the Bards, the target has Disadvantage on the save against being Charmed. Advantage + Disadvantage typically cancel out to a straight roll.

2.  **Condition Immunities:** Even though the spell *targets* a creature, it fails if the creature is immune to the **Charmed** condition.
    *   **Commonly Immune:**
        *   **Constructs** (Golems, Animated Armor, Helmed Horrors)
        *   **Undead** (Zombies, Skeletons - often mindless ones)
        *   **Oozes** (Black Pudding, Ochre Jelly - often mindless ones)
        *   **Specific Bosses** (Devils, Demons, Legendary Creatures often have resistances or immunities)
    *   **Essentially Immune:** Elves/Half-Elves/Fey have **Advantage** on saves against being Charmed (Fey Ancestry).

### Verdict for Character Build
*   **Pros:** It solves the "non-humanoid" problem. Use it on that Guard Drake, Owlbear, or Ogre that *Charm Person* can't touch.
*   **Cons:** Short range (30ft), Concentration, and combat advantage makes it risky in a fight without the Instrument.
*   **Synergy:** With an Instrument of the Bards, you can potentially Charm a non-humanoid boss (like a Young Dragon or Giant) out of combat with Disadvantage, bypassing their usual high saves.

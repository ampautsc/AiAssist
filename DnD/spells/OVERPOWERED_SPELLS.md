# Controversially Overpowered Spells in D&D 5e (2014 Rules)

This document catalogs the most controversial and mathematically overpowered spells in the 2014 ruleset (including expansions like *Xanathar's Guide to Everything* and *Tasha's Cauldron of Everything*). These spells are frequently banned, house-ruled, or targeted for nerfs in the 2024 revision because they trivialize encounters, break bounded accuracy, or abuse action economy.

---

## Cantrips

### Guidance (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Spamming "I cast Guidance" before every single ability check effectively adds +2.5 (1d4) to the entire party's skills permanently.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** It trivializes the d20 math of bounded accuracy at no resource cost.
*   **Common Fix:** Limit it to instances where the caster can reasonably predict the check, or enforce verbal component visibility (casting a spell in social situations is hostile).

### Eldritch Blast (PHB) + Agonizing Blast (Invocation)
*   **The Issue:** The best damage cantrip in the game. With the Agonizing Blast invocation, it adds CHA modifier to *each beam*.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** It scales on *character level*, not class level (a 2-level Warlock dip gives a Level 20 Paladin fully scaled ranged damage). Its force damage is rarely resisted.
*   **Controversy:** Displaces almost all other damage cantrips and incentivizes "Hexblade dips."

## 1st Level

### Silvery Barbs (Strixhaven)
*   **The Issue:** Reaction spell that forces a reroll on a success *and* gives advantage to an ally.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** It effectively negates critical hits and turns enemy successes into failures for a 1st-level slot. It is mathematically stronger than *Shield* in many cases and completely invalidates the DM's critical moments.
*   **Status:** Banned at many tables.

### Shield (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** +5 AC until the start of your next turn as a reaction.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Bounded accuracy means +5 is massive (equivalent to a Very Rare shield). A wizard with a 1-level fighter/cleric dip can easily have higher AC than the tank (Plate 18 + Shield 2 + *Shield* Spell 5 = 25 AC), making them unhittable by level-appropriate monsters.

### Goodberry (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Creates 10 berries that heal 1 HP each.
*   **Why it breaks the game:**
    1.  **Survival Trivialization:** Solves all food rations for the day with one slot.
    2.  **Life Cleric Exploit:** With Disciple of Life, each berry heals 4 HP (40 HP total for a 1st-level slot).
    3.  **Yo-Yo Healing:** You can revive 10 unconscious allies with one action's worth of berries distributed among the party.

### Find Familiar (PHB) - specifically Owl
*   **The Issue:** Owl familiar has "Flyby" (doesn't provoke opportunity attacks).
*   **Why it breaks the game:** The Owl can use the Help action every turn to give the Rogue/Paladin advantage, then fly away safely. It's a permanent source of advantage for a ritual spell.

## 2nd Level

### Pass Without Trace (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** +10 to Stealth for the entire party.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** In a system where a DC 20 check is "Hard," a +10 bonus guarantees that even the Paladin in heavy armor succeeds. It practically guarantees a Surprise Round (free round of combat) for the party in every encounter.

### Suggestion (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** "The suggestion must be worded in such a manner as to make the course of action sound reasonable."
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Extremely vague wording. Creative players can use "reasonable" phrasing to force enemies to flee, drop weapons, or turn on allies for 8 hours without concentration (in some interpretations) or just remove them from the fight entirely.

### Spike Growth (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** 2d4 damage for every 5 feet traveled.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** The "Cheese Grater" strategy. A Grappler drags an enemy back and forth through the spikes. A standard movement drag can deal 10d4 to 20d4 damage automatically with no save.

## 3rd Level

### Conjure Animals (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Summon 8 beasts (e.g., Wolves, Elk, Velociraptors).
*   **Why it breaks the game:**
    1.  **Action Economy:** 8 extra bodies on the board clog the battlefield and can surround/grapple any enemy.
    2.  **Damage:** 8 attacks (even with low to-hit) deal mathematically more damage than most martial characters.
    3.  **Slow Play:** Rolling 8 attacks slows combat to a crawl.

### Hypnotic Pattern (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Huge 30ft cube; creatures effectively incapacitated on a failed save.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** One save can end an entire encounter. If half the enemies fail, the party kills the active ones, then surrounds and executes the incapacitated ones one by one.

### Spirit Guardians (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** 3d8 damage (half on save) to enemies in 15ft radius, plus difficult terrain. Lasts 10 minutes.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** The most efficient damage-per-slot spell in the game. A Cleric can Dodge (imposing disadvantage on attacks) while melting everything around them.

### Counterspell (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Reaction to cancel a spell.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Shuts down enemy casters completely. It forces a "Counterspell war" where the only thing that matters is who has more reactions/slots. It can anticlimactically delete a boss's main ability.

### Leomund's Tiny Hut (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Impenetrable dome for 8 hours.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Allows the party to take a Long Rest safely almost anywhere in a dungeon, removing the resource attrition aspect of the game.

### Fireball (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** 8d6 damage in a huge radius.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Wizards of the Coast admitted they intentionally made *Fireball* overpowered (it should be ~6d6 by DMG guidelines) because it is "iconic." It trivializes horde encounters at level 5.

## 4th Level

### Polymorph (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Transform a creature into a beast.
*   **Why it breaks the game:**
    1.  **Offense:** Turn an ally into a Giant Ape or T-Rex (huge stats).
    2.  **Defense:** It's the best "healing" spell. A 1 HP ally becomes a 157 HP Giant Ape. When the Ape dies, the ally returns at 1 HP. It effectively adds 150+ HP buffer for a 4th-level slot.

### Conjure Woodland Beings (PHB) - specifically Pixies
*   **The Issue:** Summon 8 Pixies.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Each Pixie can cast *Polymorph*, *Fly*, or *Confusion*. You can turn the entire party into T-Rexes or Giant Apes using a single 4th-level slot by proxy commands.

## 5th Level

### Wall of Force (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Indestructible, invisible wall.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** **NO SAVE.** If you box an enemy in, they are trapped. Period. You can "microwave" them (cast persistent AOE inside, then Wall of Force) or just split the encounter in half for free.

### Animate Objects (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Animate 10 tiny objects.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** 10 attacks at +8 to hit, dealing 1d4+4 damage each. The massive number of attacks bypasses AC through sheer probability and deals absurd consistent damage (avg 65 damage/round).

---

## 6th Level

### Magic Jar (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Possess humanoid bodies indefinitely.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** A Wizard can permanently possess the body of an NPC with better physical stats (or class abilities) and keep their spellcasting. If the jar breaks, the soul interactions can instantly kill enemies without saves.

---

## 7th Level

### Forcecage (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** As *Wall of Force*, but allows spells to be cast through bars (in cage mode) or completely traps (in box mode). **No Concentration.**
*   **Why it breaks the game:** No save (unless teleporting, which requires a CHA save). No concentration means you can cast this and then cast other concentration spells (like *Sickening Radiance*) to kill the trapped target with zero risk.

### Simulacrum (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Create a copy of a beast or humanoid (yourself) with half HP but all stats and spell slots.
*   **Why it breaks the game:**
    1.  **Doubling Down:** You instantly have two full spellcasters.
    2.  **The Loop:** Your Simulacrum can technically cast *Wish* to replicate *Simulacrum* on you, creating a chain of infinite Simulacra (The "Simulacrum Chain").

---

## 8th Level

### Maze (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Banish a creature to a demiplane labyrinth.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** **NO SAVE.** To escape, the target must use an action to make a DC 20 Intelligence check (not save). Most monsters have terrible Intelligence and cannot physically succeed on a DC 20 check, removing them from combat for 10 minutes (concentration) with no counterplay.

---

## 9th Level

### Wish (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Duplicate any spell of 8th level or lower with no components/cost.
*   **Why it breaks the game:**
    1.  **Versatility:** You have access to every spell list instantly.
    2.  **Cost Bypassing:** Cast *Simulacrum* (normally 12 hours + 1500gp) instantly for free in the middle of combat. Cast *Resurrection* instantly.
    3.  **Reality Break:** The "wish for anything" clause puts immense strain on the DM to balance the game.

### True Polymorph (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** Transform creature into creature, object into creature, or creature into object. Permanent after 1 hour.
*   **Why it breaks the game:**
    1.  **Object to Creature:** Turn a rock into a CR 9 creature (like a Young Silver Dragon) loyal to you. Do this every day to build an army of dragons.
    2.  **Immortality:** Turn yourself into an immortal creature (e.g., Planetar or Dragon) permanently.

### Prismatic Wall (PHB)
*   **The Issue:** A multi-layered wall that deals massive damage and status effects.
*   **Why it breaks the game:** Can be shaped into a sphere. If you can push/pull an enemy through the wall (e.g., *Reverse Gravity* or Telekinesis), they take the damage *each time* they pass through a layer. A single pass-through can deal ~50d6 damage + petrification + blindness + banishment.

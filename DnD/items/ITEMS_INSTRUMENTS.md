**Tool Proficiency:** Musical instruments are tools. A character proficient with an instrument can add their proficiency bonus to ability checks made to play music with it. Bards start with proficiency in three instruments of their choice.

**Spellcasting Focus:** Bards can use any musical instrument as a spellcasting focus for their Bard spells.

## Magical Instruments

### Instruments of the Bards

*Source: Dungeon Master's Guide (DMG) p.176*
*Reference: https://www.dndbeyond.com/magic-items/instrument-of-the-bards*

All Instruments of the Bards share these properties:
- **Requires Attunement by a Bard**
- Grants advantage on saving throws against being charmed while playing it
- Can cast each listed spell once per day using charges
- If played while casting a spell that causes charm effects on hearing targets, targets have disadvantage on the saving throw
- A non-bard who attempts to play it must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom save or take 2d4 psychic damage

### Other Magical Instruments

#### Pipes of Haunting ⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Uncommon |
| **Requires Attunement** | No |
| **Source** | DMG p.185 |

**Description:** You must be proficient with wind instruments to use these pipes. They have 3 charges. You can use an action to play them and expend 1 charge to create an eerie, spellbinding tune. Each creature within 30 feet of you that hears you play must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened of you for 1 minute. If you wish, all creatures in the area that aren't hostile toward you automatically succeed on the saving throw. A creature that fails the saving throw can repeat it at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. A creature that succeeds on its saving throw is immune to the effect of these pipes for 24 hours. The pipes regain 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn.

---

#### Pipes of the Sewers ⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Uncommon |
| **Requires Attunement** | Yes |
| **Source** | DMG p.185 |

**Description:** You must be proficient with wind instruments to use these pipes. While you are attuned to the pipes, ordinary rats and giant rats are indifferent toward you and will not attack you unless you threaten or harm them.

The pipes have 3 charges. If you play the pipes as an action, you can use a bonus action to expend 1 to 3 charges, calling forth one swarm of rats per charge, provided that enough rats are within half a mile of you to be called in this fashion. If there aren't enough rats to form a swarm, the charge is wasted. Called swarms move toward the music by the shortest available route but aren't under your control otherwise. The pipes regain 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn.

Whenever a swarm of rats that isn't under another creature's control comes within 30 feet of you while you are playing the pipes, you can make a Charisma check contested by the swarm's Wisdom check. If you lose the contest, the swarm behaves as it normally would and can't be swayed by the pipes' music for the next 24 hours. If you win the contest, the swarm is swayed by the pipes' music and becomes friendly to you and your companions for as long as you continue to play the pipes each round as an action. A friendly swarm obeys your commands. If you issue no commands, the swarm defends itself but otherwise takes no actions.

#### Instrument of Scribing ⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Common |
| **Requires Attunement** | Yes (by a Bard) |
| **Source** | Xanathar's Guide to Everything (XGtE) p.138 |

**Description:** This musical instrument has 3 charges. While you are playing it, you can use an action to expend 1 charge from the instrument and write a magical message on a nonmagical object or surface that you can see within 30 feet of you. The message can be up to six words long and is written in a language you know. If you are a bard, you can scribe an additional number of words equal to twice your Charisma modifier. The message fades after 24 hours, or you can dismiss it as an action. The instrument regains all expended charges daily at dawn.

#### Horn of Blasting ⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Rare |
| **Requires Attunement** | No |
| **Source** | DMG p.174 |

**Description:** You can use an action to speak the horn's command word and then blow the horn, which emits a thunderous blast in a 30-foot cone that is audible 600 feet away. Each creature in the cone must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 5d6 thunder damage and is deafened for 1 minute. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage and isn't deafened. Creatures and objects made of glass or crystal have disadvantage on the saving throw and take 10d6 thunder damage instead of 5d6.

Each use of the horn's magic has a 20 percent chance of causing the horn to explode. The explosion deals 10d6 fire damage to the blower and destroys the horn.

#### Horn of Valhalla ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Varies (see below) |
| **Requires Attunement** | No |
| **Source** | DMG p.175 |

**Description:** You can use an action to blow this horn. In response, warrior spirits from the plane of Ysgard appear within 60 feet of you. They use the statistics of a berserker. They return to Ysgard after 1 hour or when they drop to 0 hit points. Once you use the horn, it can't be used again until 7 days have passed.

| Horn Type | Rarity | Spirits Summoned | Requirement |
|-----------|--------|------------------|-------------|
| Silver | Rare | 2d4 + 2 | None |
| Brass | Rare | 3d4 + 3 | Proficiency with all simple weapons |
| Bronze | Very Rare | 4d4 + 4 | Proficiency with all medium armor |
| Iron | Legendary | 5d4 + 5 | Proficiency with all martial weapons |

If you blow the horn without meeting its requirement, the summoned berserkers attack you. If you meet the requirement, they are friendly to you and your companions and follow your commands.

#### Instrument of Illusions ⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Common |
| **Requires Attunement** | Yes |
| **Source** | Xanathar's Guide to Everything (XGtE) p.137 |

**Description:** While you are playing this musical instrument, you can create harmless, illusory visual effects within a 5-foot-radius sphere centered on the instrument. If you are a bard, the radius increases to 15 feet. Sample visual effects include luminous musical notes, a spectral dancer, butterflies, and gently falling snow. The magical effects have neither combative capabilities nor harmful effects, and they are obviously illusory. The effects end when you stop playing.

#### Gjallarhorn ⭐⭐⭐

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Type** | Wondrous item (instrument) |
| **Rarity** | Legendary |
| **Requires Attunement** | Yes |
| **Source** | Candlekeep Mysteries p.190 |

**Description:** Crafted from the horn of a goat, this legendary horn is said to be able to wake the gods. It has the following properties:

- **Innate Spellcasting:** The horn has 3 charges. While holding it, you can use an action to expend 1 or more charges to cast one of the following spells (save DC 18): *compelled duel* (1 charge), *freedom of movement* (2 charges), or *legend lore* (3 charges). The horn regains 1d3 expended charges daily at dawn.
- **Divine Alert:** As an action, you can blow the horn and produce a thunderous blast that can be heard 1,000 feet away. In addition, any celestial within 1,000 feet that hears the horn is compelled to investigate.
- **Fearsome Presence:** While holding the horn, you can use an action to make yourself appear fearsome. Each creature of your choice within 30 feet of you must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened of you for 1 minute.

## Quick Reference: Best Magic Instruments by Tier

| Tier | Recommended Instrument | Why |
|------|------------------------|-----|
| **Tier 1 (1-4)** | Instrument of Illusions (Common) | Cheap, adds flair, no attunement waste |
| **Tier 2 (5-10)** | Doss Lute / Mac-Fuirmidh Cittern (Uncommon) | Free Fly, Invisibility once/day |
| **Tier 2 Alt** | Rhythm-Maker's Drum +1 (Uncommon) | Spell DC boost + recover Bardic Inspiration |
| **Tier 3 (11-16)** | Canaith Mandolin / Cli Lyre (Rare) | Better spell selection |
| **Tier 3 Alt** | Reveler's Concertina (Rare) | +2 DC is powerful |
| **Tier 4 (17-20)** | Ollamh Harp (Legendary) | Fire Storm, Control Weather — huge spells |
| **Tier 4 Alt** | Rhythm-Maker's Drum +3 (Very Rare) | +3 DC is the highest available |

## 🗡️ COMBAT ANALYSIS — HEAD TO HEAD

### The Save-or-Suck Equation

This is where the rubber meets the road. Both Bards live and die by landing their control spells.

**Eloquence Bard — Unsettling Words:**
- Bonus action: subtract 1d6 (avg 3.5) → 1d8 (avg 4.5) → 1d10 (avg 5.5) → 1d12 (avg 6.5) from enemy's NEXT save
- At level 10 with d10 BI: effectively **+5.5 to your spell save DC** for one target
- Stacks with Silvery Barbs (reaction for disadvantage on save)
- Refreshes on short rest (Font of Inspiration at level 5)
- Average effective DC with 20 CHA at level 10: **17 base + 5.5 = ~22-23 effective DC**
- The optimization community calls this "bounded-accuracy-breaking" — it's like having 5 Portents per short rest

**Lore Bard — Cutting Words:**
- Reaction: subtract 1d6→d12 from attack roll, ability check, or damage roll
- More versatile targets (attacks, checks, damage) but doesn't boost YOUR spells
- Can't be used proactively — only after enemy rolls
- Also refreshes on short rest
- Great for protecting concentration (reduce damage → easier CON save)
- Can subtract from enemy's Counterspell check against your spells!

**VERDICT:** Eloquence wins the save-or-suck war decisively. Being able to increase your own spell DC by 4-6 is like having a Sorcerer's Heightened Spell metamagic for free on a short rest resource. Cutting Words is more versatile but less impactful on the thing that matters most: landing your big spells.

### Spell Access Comparison

This is Lore's biggest advantage — Additional Magical Secrets at level 6.

**Lore Bard's Additional Magical Secrets (Level 6):**
Can steal up to 3rd-level spells from ANY class. Top picks:

| Spell | Source | Why It's Amazing |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **Counterspell** | Wizard | Shut down enemy casters; Jack of All Trades applies to the check |
| **Fireball** | Wizard | Only reliable AoE damage option for Bard at this level |
| **Spirit Guardians** | Cleric | Incredible sustained damage in melee-heavy parties |
| **Aura of Vitality** | Paladin | 20d6 healing over 1 minute (out of combat healing machine) |
| **Pass Without Trace** | Druid | +10 to party Stealth = guaranteed surprise rounds |
| **Web** | Wizard | Excellent low-level control; restrained condition is brutal |
| **Haste** | Wizard | Double a martial's effectiveness |
| **Revivify** | Cleric | Resurrection insurance if no cleric in party |
| **Sleet Storm** | Druid | No-save difficult terrain + concentration breaker |

**Eloquence Bard's Magical Secrets (Level 10 only):**
Must wait until level 10 for first Magical Secrets (up to 5th-level spells). Standard picks:

| Spell | Source | Why It's Amazing |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **Wall of Force** | Wizard | Auto-pick. No save, no concentration issues. Divides fights. |
| **Find Greater Steed** | Paladin | Permanent flying mount (Pegasus). Incredible mobility. |
| **Counterspell** | Wizard | If no one else has it |
| **Pass Without Trace** | Druid | If no one else has it |
| **Conjure Animals** | Druid | Action economy powerhouse |

**The Level 6-9 Gap:**
- Lore Bard has **Counterspell + Spirit Guardians** (or similar) from level 6
- Eloquence Bard has **Unsettling Words making Hypnotic Pattern land 20%+ more often**
- This is a genuine trade-off. The community is split, but the consensus leans Eloquence because Unsettling Words is a short-rest resource that applies to EVERY spell, while Additional Magical Secrets is a one-time pickup

### Bardic Inspiration Economy

**How You Spend Your Inspiration Dice:**

| Usage | Lore Bard | Eloquence Bard |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Give to ally (attack/save/check)** | Primary use | Secondary use |
| **Cutting Words (reaction)** | ✅ Can subtract from enemy rolls | ❌ Not available |
| **Unsettling Words (bonus action)** | ❌ Not available | ✅ Subtract from enemy saves |
| **Peerless Skill (self, level 14)** | ✅ Boost own ability checks | ❌ Not available |
| **If ally fails with it** | ❌ Die is wasted | ✅ They KEEP the die (Unfailing Inspiration) |
| **If ally succeeds with it** | Nothing special | ✅ Give it to SOMEONE ELSE (Infectious Inspiration) |

**Eloquence's Inspiration Economy is insane:**
- Use Unsettling Words to make your spells land → primary use
- If you DO give inspiration to an ally and they fail → they keep it → try again
- If they succeed with it → give it to someone else → infinite value chain
- Net result: Eloquence gets dramatically more value per Bardic Inspiration die

**Lore's Inspiration Economy is more flexible:**
- Cutting Words works on attacks (save allies), checks (counter enemy grapples), AND damage (reduce burst)
- Peerless Skill at 14 means you can boost your own Counterspell/Dispel Magic checks
- More reactive/defensive, but genuinely versatile

## 🎯 THE CRUX OF IT — CONCENTRATION vs SAVE-OR-SUCK

> **Core Insight:** Cutting Words is effectively an AC boost — subtract from enemy attack rolls → they miss you → your concentration holds. Unsettling Words is effectively a DC boost — subtract from enemy saves → your save-or-suck spells land. **Lore Bards are concentration casters. Eloquence Bards are save-or-suck casters.** This is the fundamental strategic divergence that should drive every spell selection, feat choice, and tactical decision.

### Why This Matters

**Lore Bard's Defensive Loop:**
1. Cast a powerful concentration spell (Hypnotic Pattern, Haste, Spirit Guardians)
2. Enemy attacks you → use **Cutting Words** to subtract d6-d12 from their attack roll
3. Attack misses → you never take damage → you never make a CON save → concentration holds
4. Your concentration spell continues dominating the battlefield
5. Effectively: **Cutting Words = +3.5 to +6.5 AC** against one attack per round

**Eloquence Bard's Offensive Loop:**
1. Use **Unsettling Words** as a bonus action (subtract d6-d12 from enemy's next save)
2. Cast a save-or-suck spell (Hypnotic Pattern, Hold Person, Suggestion)
3. Enemy's save is reduced by 3.5-6.5 on average → spell lands far more often
4. One landed save-or-suck spell can end an encounter
5. Effectively: **Unsettling Words = +3.5 to +6.5 spell save DC** on one target

**The Trade-off:**
- Lore plays **defensive/sustained** — keep your buff/control running all fight
- Eloquence plays **offensive/burst** — land the fight-ending spell immediately
- Spells that are BOTH concentration AND save-or-suck (like Hypnotic Pattern) benefit from either approach, but through different mechanics

## 🧠 OUT OF COMBAT — SOCIAL & EXPLORATION

### The Social Pillar

**Eloquence Bard with Expertise:**
- **Silver Tongue minimum Persuasion at level 10:** 10 + 5 (CHA) + 4 (Prof) + 4 (Expertise) = **23 minimum**
- **With 20 CHA:** 10 + 5 + 4 + 4 = **minimum 23, max 37**
- You literally **cannot roll below a 23** on Persuasion
- **Deception works the same way** — minimum 23
- **Universal Speech** at level 6 removes all language barriers
- This is arguably the best "face" character possible in 5e

**Lore Bard with Expertise:**
- Normal roll: 1 + 5 + 4 + 4 = **minimum 14, max 33**
- At level 14, can add **Peerless Skill** (d10-d12) to own checks
- With Peerless Skill + Expertise: average roll on critical social check is ~30
- Has 3 extra skills from Bonus Proficiencies for broader coverage
- Jack of All Trades helps everything else

**VERDICT:** Eloquence is the undisputed king of social encounters. The floor of 23+ on every Persuasion/Deception check is unmatched. Lore is broader (more skills) but Eloquence is deeper (higher guaranteed minimums on CHA checks).

### Skill Coverage

| Feature | Lore Bard | Eloquence Bard |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **Skill Proficiencies** | 3 (Bard) + 2 (Background) + **3 (Bonus)** = **8 total** | 3 (Bard) + 2 (Background) = **5 total** |
| **Expertise (by 10)** | 4 skills doubled | 4 skills doubled |
| **Jack of All Trades** | +half prof to unproficient | +half prof to unproficient |
| **Silver Tongue** | ❌ | ✅ Floor of 10 on Persuasion/Deception |
| **Peerless Skill (14)** | ✅ Add BI to own checks | ❌ |

Lore Bard is proficient in **3 more skills** — that's significant for out-of-combat exploration, investigation, and social breadth.

| Unsettling Words (Elo 3) | ❌ | ✅ +3.5 effective DC on single target |
| **Edge** | Lore slightly — more skills, Cutting Words protects the squishy party | Eloquence slightly — Unsettling Words + Suggestion is a combo |

### Tier 2 (Levels 5-10) — ⚡ THE CRITICAL DIVERGENCE

| Factor | Lore Bard | Eloquence Bard |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **LEVEL 6 SPIKE** | 🔥 **Additional Magical Secrets** — Counterspell + Pass Without Trace (or Web, Spirit Guardians, Fireball) | Unfailing Inspiration (nice, not game-changing) + Universal Speech |
| Font of Inspiration (5) | Unsettling Words on every big spell | Same |
| Hypnotic Pattern | Great with Cutting Words to protect concentration | AMAZING with Unsettling Words (+4.5 to DC) |
| Level 10 Secrets | Wall of Force + Find Greater Steed | Same |
| **Edge** | **Lore from 6-9** — those extra spells are a massive power spike | **Eloquence from 5-6 and 10+** — Unsettling Words scales incredibly |

**The Level 6-9 Window is Lore's Golden Age:**
A Lore Bard at level 6 with Counterspell + Pass Without Trace has access to spells that Eloquence won't see until level 10-14. This is a real, significant advantage that lasts for 4 crucial levels of play. Many campaigns live in this tier.

**But Eloquence catches up hard at 10:**
By level 10, Eloquence has d10 Unsettling Words (+5.5 effective DC), Unfailing Inspiration, and the same Magical Secrets. The gap closes and reverses.

## 🎯 SPECIFIC SCENARIO COMPARISONS

### Scenario 1: Landing Hypnotic Pattern on a Boss (Level 10)

**Setup:** DC 17 save, Boss has +5 WIS save

| Bard | Boss Needs | Success Rate | Method |
|------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Eloquence** | 17 + 5.5 (avg UW) = effectively DC 22.5 | Boss needs 17.5+ on d20 → **~17% save chance** | Unsettling Words (BA) then Hypnotic Pattern |
| **Lore** | 17 flat | Boss needs 12+ on d20 → **~45% save chance** | Just cast Hypnotic Pattern |
| **Lore + Silvery Barbs** | 17, disadvantage if fails | ~45% first, then ~20% on reroll → **~20% total save chance** | Cast HP, spend reaction + 1st level slot on SB |

Eloquence gets comparable results to Lore + Silvery Barbs but without spending a reaction or spell slot — just a short-rest BI die.

### Scenario 2: Counterspelling a Dangerous Spell (Level 10)

**Setup:** Enemy casts 6th-level spell, you Counterspell at 3rd → need DC 16 check

| Bard | Check Bonus | Success Rate | Notes |
|------|------------|-------------|-------|
| **Lore (Level 14+ with Peerless Skill)** | +7 (CHA + JoaT) + d10 (avg 5.5) = ~+12.5 | ~82% success | Uses BI die but very reliable |
| **Eloquence** | +7 (CHA + JoaT) = +7 | ~60% success | No self-buff option |
| **Lore (Level 10, no Peerless)** | +7 = +7 | ~60% success | Same as Eloquence at this level |

**VERDICT:** Lore has the edge on Counterspell/Dispel Magic from level 14+ thanks to Peerless Skill.

### Scenario 3: Convincing the Hostile Duke (Out of Combat)

**Setup:** Hostile NPC, DC 25 Persuasion check

| Bard | Minimum Roll | Average Roll | Max Roll | Auto-Success? |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|--------------|
| **Eloquence (20 CHA, Expertise, Level 10)** | 10+5+4+4 = **23** | 15.5+13 = **28.5** | 20+13 = **33** | Hits 23+ guaranteed; 25+ = 85% |
| **Lore (20 CHA, Expertise, Level 10)** | 1+5+4+4 = **14** | 10.5+13 = **23.5** | 20+13 = **33** | Needs 12+ on d20 = 55% |
| **Lore (Level 14 + Peerless Skill)** | 1+5+5+5+1 = **17** | ~29 | 20+14+12 = **46** | Peerless brings it to ~80%+ |

Eloquence dominates social encounters at every level. Silver Tongue turns inconsistency into inevitability.

### Scenario 4: Protecting the Wizard's Concentration (Combat)

**Setup:** Dragon hits wizard for 30 damage, wizard needs DC 15 CON save

| Bard | Method | Save Improvement | Resource Cost |
|------|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Lore — Cutting Words** | Subtract d10 from dragon's attack roll (might cause miss entirely) | Prevents damage entirely if miss; or subtract from damage to lower DC | 1 BI (reaction) |
| **Eloquence** | Give wizard BI die to add to CON save | +5.5 avg to save | 1 BI (given previously as bonus action) |

**VERDICT:** Lore is actually better here — preventing or reducing damage is more efficient than boosting the save after the fact. Cutting Words can completely negate the hit.

## 🔄 FOR LUCKY'S BUILD SPECIFICALLY

Lucky is currently designed as an **Eloquence Bard 10** focused on the Simulacrum merchant network. Here's how each subclass would serve Lucky's concept:

### Eloquence Advantages for Lucky ✅

|--------|-------|

### Verdict for Lucky: **Eloquence is the right call** 🏆
The Silver Tongue floor of 27 on Persuasion/Deception is tailor-made for a trade network operator. No amount of extra skills or early Magical Secrets competes with "I literally cannot fail a Persuasion check below DC 27." Combined with Universal Speech removing language barriers and Unfailing Inspiration making simulacrum-given BI never wasted, Eloquence is Lucky's subclass, no contest.

## 🏁 FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### Choose **LORE BARD** if:
- ✅ Your party has gaps that need filling NOW (levels 6-9)
- ✅ You're the only full caster and need maximum spell versatility
- ✅ You want 3 extra skills for out-of-combat breadth
- ✅ Campaign ends around level 10-12 (Lore's power spike at 6 is maximally impactful)
- ✅ You want reactive protection (Cutting Words is excellent defense)
- ✅ You value Peerless Skill for self-buffing Counterspell/Dispel
- ✅ You're playing a "Swiss Army Knife" generalist

### Choose **ELOQUENCE BARD** if:
- ✅ You want the BEST save-or-suck debuffer in the game
- ✅ Social encounters are a major part of your campaign
- ✅ You want guaranteed social check floors (never roll below 23+ Persuasion)
- ✅ Campaign goes to high levels (Eloquence scales harder)
- ✅ Your party already has other casters covering Counterspell/healing/etc.
- ✅ You're building a "Face" character who needs to be unbeatable in conversation
- ✅ You plan to multiclass (Elo's power is frontloaded at level 3)
- ✅ You're running a Simulacrum network (Lucky!) — the inspiration economy is insane

### The Optimization Community Consensus:
> **Eloquence is the stronger subclass overall**, but **Lore is more versatile and better in certain party compositions**. Both are S-tier. You can't go wrong with either.

> *"Unsettling Words is bounded-accuracy-breakingly strong. In my own experiences playing the class in higher levels, it often felt akin to having 5 Portents per short rest."* — kobo1d (Tabletop Builds)

> *"Lore is quite competitive in general due to extremely potent Magical Secrets options like Pass Without Trace and Web."* — Audere (Tabletop Builds)

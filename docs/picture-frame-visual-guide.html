🔲 The Rabbet (Groove)

The rabbet is a step cut into the back of the frame that holds glass, mat, artwork, and backing:

⅜" depth
⅜" width

Cross-Section View

The rabbet creates a "shelf" on the back edge of the frame where all your materials sit:

  • Glass rests against the front edge
  • Mat board goes next
  • Artwork sits on the mat
  • Backing board fills the rest
  • Glazier points hold everything in

Standard size: ⅜" × ⅜" works for most frames

1

Dry Fit First

Put all four pieces together WITHOUT glue to make sure everything fits and corners are tight.

2

Apply Glue

Spread wood glue on BOTH faces of each miter joint. A thin, even coat is better than gobs.

3

Clamp & Align

Use corner clamps or a band clamp. Wipe excess glue immediately with a damp cloth.

4

Wait 24 Hours

Let the glue cure completely before removing clamps. Patience = strong joints!

📚 Assembly Order (Front to Back)

Stack materials in this order from the front of the frame to the back:

1
GLASS
Glass or Acrylic — Clean both sides thoroughly. No fingerprints!
2
MAT BOARD
Mat Board — Must be acid-free. Window cut 2-3" smaller than frame.
3
ARTWORK
Artwork — Mounted with photo corners or acid-free hinge tape.
4
BACKING BOARD
Backing — Acid-free foam board or corrugated. Cut to fit snugly.

🔒 Step 5: SEAL THE PACKAGE (Recommended!)

For maximum protection, seal the entire stack with acid-free framing tape before putting it in the frame:

Framing Tape

How to seal:

  1. Stack glass + mat + art + backing
  2. Align all edges perfectly
  3. Wrap acid-free framing tape around ALL 4 edges
  4. Tape covers edge of glass AND backing
  5. Press firmly for airtight seal
  6. Drop sealed package into frame
✅ What Framing Tape Protects:
  • Dust & insects
  • Humidity changes
  • Air pollutants
  • Normal indoor conditions
🛒 Recommended Tapes:
  • Lineco Frame Sealing Tape
  • Filmoplast P90
  • 3M ATG (acid-free)
  • Aluminum tape (moisture barrier)

⚠️ Standard Materials are NOT Waterproof!

Both framing tape AND standard backing absorb water. For true waterproofing, you need waterproof materials for BOTH.

❌ NOT Waterproof (Absorbs Water):
  • Acid-free foam board
  • Corrugated cardboard
  • Mat board
  • Standard framing tape
✅ Natural Waterproof Options:
  • Birch plywood - sealed with tung oil
  • Hardboard/Masonite - sealed with shellac
  • Cork board - sealed with linseed oil

All natural materials need proper sealing!

🌊 Complete Waterproof Recipe:

Glass
Artwork
Plastic
  1. Use waterproof backing (Coroplast or Sintra)
  2. Stack: Glass → Mat → Art → Plastic backing
  3. Seal edges with silicone caulk or butyl tape
  4. Let cure 24 hours

Both are required! Waterproof backing + waterproof edge seal = truly waterproof frame

🔨 How to Seal Wood Backing:
  1. Cut ⅛" birch plywood to size
  2. Sand smooth with 220 grit
  3. Apply tung oil: brush on, wipe excess after 15 min
  4. Repeat 3-4 coats (24 hours between coats)
  5. Let cure 48 hours → waterproof!

📌 Securing with Glazier Points

Glazier points are small metal tabs pushed into the frame to hold everything in place:

Back of Frame View

Place glazier points every 3-4 inches around the frame.

  • Use a flat screwdriver or glazier point driver
  • Push points into the wood at an angle
  • Points should press firmly on backing
  • Don't push too hard—you can crack glass!

Alternative: Turn buttons are easier and allow the frame to be opened later.

⚠️ Safety Reminders

👓

Eye Protection

ALWAYS wear safety glasses when using power tools or cutting glass.

🧤

Gloves for Glass

Wear gloves when handling glass. Edges can be razor sharp.

🌬️

Ventilation

Work in a well-ventilated area when using stains, finishes, and paints.

🔥

Oily Rags

Oil-soaked rags can self-combust! Lay flat to dry or store in water.
